Annotation

  • System routes patient to the consultation form for that product category (e.g. Hair Loss)
  • The product and category are passed through so the correct form loads
  • No login or account required at this stage — anyone can start a consultation
  • Account creation / login is only required later at checkout (Phase 3)

Annotation

  • Forms are configurable in the backend — questions, order, and logic rules per category
  • Questions cover: medical history, current medications, allergies, symptoms, previous treatments
  • Auto-check logic runs on each answer in real time
  • Example disqualifier: patient selects a contraindicated medical condition
  • Auto-denial happens before payment is taken — no refund needed
  • Patient may resubmit — see Phase 10 (repeat submission detection)

Consent

  • Before submission, patient must tick consent: T&Cs acceptance + agreement to remote consultation
  • Consent is also required at reorder — patient confirms they can continue taking the treatment
  • After subscription ends or the 6-month hard limit, a full new consultation form is required

Abandonment recovery

  • If a logged-in patient starts but does not complete the consultation, the system logs the incomplete session
  • After a configurable timeframe (e.g. 1 hour / 24 hours), system sends a reminder email: "You started a consultation but didn't finish — pick up where you left off"
  • Only works for logged-in users (we need their email to send the reminder)
  • Non-logged-in abandonments cannot be recovered (no contact details available)

Annotation

Login / account creation

  • Patient completes the consultation without needing an account
  • Account is only required at checkout — reduces friction on the consultation form
  • If already logged in, they skip straight to payment
  • New account collects: full name, DOB, email, phone, delivery address
  • Consultation record is linked to the account once created/logged in

Payment model

  • Payment is taken upfront, in full — not a pre-auth hold
  • If the order is later rejected (ID fail or clinical denial), a full refund is issued
  • Auto-denial (Phase 2) happens before payment — no refund needed in that case

Record saved

  • Patient ID, all Q&As (mapped to questions), timestamp, product/category, unique reference number
  • Both POM and P items go through ID check next — review role differs later

Patient cancellation (pre-dispatch)

  • Patient has a "Request Cancellation" button in their account — available for a limited time window after payment (before dispatch)
  • Button sends a message/email to the pharmacy team via the messaging centre
  • Team manually processes the cancellation and issues a refund
  • This is a manual process — not an instant auto-cancel

Annotation

Notification email (secure, limited details)

  • Contains: consultation reference, product category, type (POM/P), date/time, direct link to case
  • Does NOT contain: patient name, DOB, answers, or any clinical information
  • Prescriber must log in to see anything — link goes to the specific case
  • Notification preferences configurable: immediate, batched (every 2h), or daily digest

Denial

  • Mandatory: select reason from predefined list + free-text clinical notes
  • Patient rejection email sent only after refund is confirmed

Consultation timeout / expiry

  • System flags consultations pending review for more than 7 days — visible in dashboard
  • If clinician requested more info and patient hasn't replied — re-flagged after 7 days
  • After 30 days of inactivity, order is deemed "abandoned" — pharmacy team can manually reject and refund
  • Consultations are flagged, not auto-expired — the team makes the final call

Annotation

Where things happen

  • Vivamen backend: treatment review, patient notes, clinical decision
  • SignatureRx: the prescription itself is created, written, and signed

API flow (two calls — one out, one back)

  • Website → SignatureRx: CREATE PRESCRIPTION — sends all Rx fields, receives reference ID
  • SignatureRx → Website: WEBHOOK — sends signed Rx details, SHA-256 hash, timestamp, prescriber details
  • Everything between these calls (MFA, signing) is handled entirely within SignatureRx

Rx fields (auto-populated)

Patient name, DOB, medication name, strength, form, dose, frequency, quantity/duration, directions, date of issue, prescriber identity. Prescriber can adjust any field except name and DOB.

Annotation

Delivery failure

  • Failed deliveries (returned to sender, refused, wrong address) go into a dedicated "Delivery Failed" screen
  • Pharmacy team is notified via email when a delivery fails
  • Team can: re-dispatch to corrected address, contact patient, or issue refund

Reorder flow

  • Patient can reorder the same treatment after a configurable timeframe based on the product (e.g. 1-month supply → reorder available after ~2 weeks)
  • Reorder requires consent confirmation — patient agrees they can continue taking the treatment
  • Reorder skips the consultation form — goes straight to payment → dispatch
  • After the subscription ends OR the 6-month hard limit, a full new consultation form is required

Audit trail (accessible to clinical team at any time)

  • Consultation form and answers
  • ID check result
  • Clinical decision (approve/deny), reason, notes, clinician ID
  • Prescription record and signature details (hash, timestamp, prescriber)
  • Payment record + refund status (if applicable)
  • Dispatch and tracking info
